Jules Mitchell

About

I am a first year PhD student at the Thompson Institute, Sunshine Coast. My thesis will investigate the safety and tolerability of oral low dose ketamine and zinc supplementation in people living with Anorexia Nervosa. Both ketamine and zinc target the NMDA receptor; influencing synaptic plasticity, and this is associated with therapeutic effects in depression and suicidality. My primary interest is in investigating the changes in brain neurotransmitters, including glutamate and GABA, using magnetic resonance spectroscopy and the association they have with changes in behavioural and cognitive symptoms. Additionally, blood biomarkers of central cellular regulators of metabolism and synaptic plasticity (SIRT1 and mTOR) will be measured to ascertain the cellular pathways altered by ketamine and zinc in Anorexia Nervosa.
